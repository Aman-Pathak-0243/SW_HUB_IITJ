## 1. Your personal account

Access to the Member Platform is by **institute account** — you sign in with your **institute email and a password**. Accounts are **provisioned by administrators**, so you do not create one yourself; if you do not have one yet, you can **request one right from the portal** (see [How to get started](#8-how-to-get-started)).

On your **first login** you will be asked to **set your own password** before you go any further. This keeps your account yours. If you ever forget your password, there is a **Forgot password** option on the sign-in page — it sends a request to the team, who will help you reset it. For your safety, the portal never reveals whether an email is or is not registered.

---

## 2. Your member area

Once you sign in, you land in your **member area** — your home on the platform:

- **Your profile** — your identity, your role(s), and a running view of your **event involvement** and any **achievements** credited to you.
- **My clubs** — the clubs, societies, and chapters you belong to, so you can jump straight to their pages.
- **Your achievements** — the recognitions credited to you on the Wall of Fame.

Your profile is a **personal, private view** — it is shown to you when you are signed in, and your personal details stay protected.

---

## 3. The centralized Event Playground

This is the heart of the new platform: **one place for every event**, from any club, council, or organising team.

- **Discover events** — browse what is coming up and open any event to read the full details, problem statement, and eligibility.
- **Register in a click** — sign up for an event you are eligible for. (Registration is **login-only**, so you will need to be signed in with an active account.)
- **Waitlists that just work** — if an event is already full, you join the **waitlist** automatically. If a seat frees up, the **next person on the waitlist is promoted automatically** — no need to keep refreshing.
- **Rounds and stages** — multi-round events (quizzes, hackathons, case studies, and more) show each round clearly.
- **Live rankings** — follow standings **per round and overall** as scores come in.

Find it at **`/events`** once you are signed in.

---

## 4. The Wall of Fame

The **Wall of Fame** is where the institute celebrates its people. It is a curated showcase of **student and club achievements**, each one credited to the member or the club behind it. Browse it any time at **`/wall-of-fame`** — and keep an eye out for your own name.

---

## 5. Club & council pages

Every club and council now has a rich, **tabbed page** that brings everything together in one place:

- **Overview** — vision, details, and the team (including custom team roles).
- **Announcements** — the club's own updates, some of which also appear on the central board.
- **Upcoming & past events** — what is coming and what has already happened.
- **Achievements** — the club's slice of the Wall of Fame.
- **Resources & documents** — files and reference material the club shares.
- **Members** — the people who make it happen.

These pages are the public face of each club and council — a living record, not a static brochure.

---

## 6. For coordinators: run your own events and manage members

Club and council **coordinators** get a dedicated back office at **`/coordinator`** — a focused workspace for **your own unit**, without needing full admin access. From there you can:

- **Run your events end to end** — set capacity and the registration window, add rounds, manage your registration roster (add, change status, or remove registrants), record **scores and attendance**, submit your own **closure report**, and **download CSV** files (participants, scores, attendance, rankings).
- **Manage your members** — view your roster, add or update members, and run a **non-destructive bulk CSV import** (it never silently overwrites roles or creates accounts on its own).
- **See your club's contribution** — a summary of your unit's activity and impact over the year.

You only ever see and manage **your own club or council** — the platform keeps everything neatly scoped to the units you coordinate.

---

## 7. Feedback & support

Found a bug? Have a suggestion? Use the **feedback & support** channel at **`/feedback`**. Every submission gets a **unique reference ID** so you (and the team) can track it from open through to resolved. This is the fastest way to tell us what is working, what is not, and what you would love to see next.

---

## 8. How to get started

1. **Go to the portal and open the sign-in page** at **`/login`**.
2. **Already have an account?** Sign in with your **institute email and password**. **New here?** Choose **Request an account** (`/account/request`) — an administrator will provision one for you and share your initial password through official institute mail.
3. **Set your own password on first login.** The portal will prompt you to change the initial password before you continue — pick something strong and memorable.
4. **Explore your member area** — check your profile, find your clubs under **My clubs**, and see any achievements credited to you.
5. **Head to the Event Playground** (`/events`) to discover events, register, and follow rankings.
6. **Coordinators:** open **`/coordinator`** to run your events and manage your members.
7. **Forgot your password?** Use **Forgot password** on the sign-in page (`/account/forgot`) and the team will help you reset it.

---

## 9. Good to know

- **Access is by institute account.** You sign in with your institute email; accounts are set up by administrators, and you can request one from the portal.
- **Some features are login-only.** Discovering events is open, but **registering, your profile, and your member area require you to be signed in** with an active account.
- **Your first login asks for a password change** — this is a one-time step to secure your account.
- **The public site is still here.** If you are just browsing, the familiar pages remain — the new member features simply open up once you sign in.
